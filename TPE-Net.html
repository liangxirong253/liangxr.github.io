📖 Abstract

Underwater images are crucial for marine applications but often suffer from degradation caused by wavelength-dependent light absorption and scattering. The lack of high-quality paired data remains a key bottleneck, as synthetic data poorly reflect real underwater complexity and real-world datasets often rely on algorithm-generated references with color deviations, artifacts, or detail loss. To address this issue, we propose TPE-Net, a terrestrial codebook prior-guided underwater image enhancement framework via compositional supervision. TPE-Net exploits a pretrained terrestrial codebook as a compact and queryable discrete representation space, providing stable natural color and texture priors to compensate for imperfect underwater references. The compositional supervision is formulated as a two-stage scheme centered on contrastive learning. In the first stage, contrastive constraints are imposed within the finite codebook-based representation space to suppress degradation-related features and achieve global enhancement, preventing unconstrained feature separation. In the second stage, a confidence predictor and a distance-based criterion assess regional restoration quality, allowing well-restored regions to guide the refinement of locally degraded areas. Extensive experiments demonstrate that TPE-Net delivers prior-guided enhancement with favorable perceptual quality and task-level benefits across diverse underwater benchmarks.

Index Terms — Underwater image enhancement, Terrestrial codebook prior, Compositional supervision, Contrastive learning.

✨ Highlights

  • 1) Terrestrial-pretrained codebook prior — A terrestrial-pretrained codebook is introduced for underwater enhancement, providing a finite and queryable discrete prior space.
  • 2) Codebook-constrained contrastive learning — Codebook-constrained contrastive learning suppresses global degradation within a bounded prior representation space.
  • 3) Confidence-guided local refinement — Confidence-guided refinement enables spatially adaptive local correction and improves restoration consistency.
  • 4) Perceptual and task-level benefits — Experiments verify favorable perceptual quality and downstream task benefits across diverse underwater benchmarks.
